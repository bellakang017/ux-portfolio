Analysis
Key Insights

Authenticity is the core vulnerability

The largest perception gap (Δ=0.56) was on "authentic" — not trust, not reliability. Users detect inauthenticity before they consciously distrust. For AI personas, perceived authenticity is the first gate.

Attitude shift ≠ behavior shift

Despite a substantial attitude gap (Δ=0.39), purchase intention barely moved (Δ=0.16). Negative perception of virtual influencers doesn't translate linearly to rejection. The attitude-behavior gap suggests other factors mediate purchase decisions.

Polarization vs. neutrality pattern

Virtual condition responses polarized toward disagreement (61.8% negative on credibility). Human condition clustered at neutral (40.4%). Virtual influencers don't just perform worse — they provoke stronger reactions.

Design implication for AI agents

If AI-generated personas trigger credibility penalties even in passive content (video ads), the effect is likely amplified in interactive contexts (chatbots, AI agents). Disclosure framing and authenticity signals become critical design variables.
